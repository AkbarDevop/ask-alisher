Official page brief based on a February 4, 2026 Youth Affairs Agency news item.

A delegation of Uzbek youth returned from Germany after studying legal work opportunities, the living conditions of Uzbek students, and ways to improve the "Work and Travel" program.

The visit focused on real problems students face abroad, including income, residence, and legal issues. Delegation members also spoke with partner organizations, German farmers, and company representatives to understand how to create safer and more lawful work opportunities for Uzbek youth.

The resulting proposals aimed to build a more convenient and secure system for young people who want to work or travel abroad. This source is useful for questions about international mobility, student work programs, practical safety issues, and the Agency's international cooperation agenda.
